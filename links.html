  • Radical Constructivism: A Way of Knowing and Learning
    • A book explaining a theory of how we come to know things. I have found this quite useful when thinking about knowledge representation in computers.
  • Laws of Form
    • Takes the idea of distinction and uses it to create a complete mathematical system. The interesting part of this book, for me, lies in the first few pages which describe the axioms of the system. I recommend thinking about Bongard problems while you read.
  • Arrival (2016 movie)
    • Kakoi's visuals were inspired in part by the alien language. Also, if you liked the movie, you really aught to read the book it's based on. The movie takes, in my oppinion, certain wrong interpretations of the source material that lead it away from hard science fiction and towards fantasy.
  • Fexprs as the basis of Lisp function application or $vau: the ultimate abstraction
    • A paper that describes an extremely customizable programming language.
  • IPFS: the InterPlanetary File System
    • A decentralized file system in which the information pointed to by a link does not change.
  • Kakoi definition
    • Kakoi is a word used when playing the board game Go to describe a move that acts to surround an area. I named the project Kakoi because the main action taken in the system is to surround alike things.
  • How do mathematicians learn math?: resources and acts for constructing and understanding mathematics
    • A paper that investigates what is necessary for learning mathematical topics.
  • Zettelkasten
    • A "knowledge management and note-taking" system. A major inspiration for Kakoi.
  • Org-roam
    • Zettelkasten, but for Emacs' org-mode. What's not to like?
